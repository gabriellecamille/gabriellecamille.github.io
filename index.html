Biography

Hello! My name is Gabrielle Ferreira, and I am a researcher with a PhD in Media and Communication from the University of Oslo. I have a genuine passion for television and audiences, and my main expertise lies in understanding what makes people connect with the content they watch. Combining qualitative and quantitative methods, I make sense of complex data across multiple markets to uncover broader trends.

For my PhD project, I investigated the transformations in how television content travels across borders, using the expansion of Turkish dramas in Western Europe as a case study. Findings reveal profound changes on both the industry and audience sides, drawing attention to the dynamics behind the rise of new players in the global television market, and to the growing interest among viewers in content that showcases cultural difference rather than cultural proximity.

Previously, I investigated how Turkish dramas were replacing Latin American telenovelas by mapping out emerging audience patterns in Brazil and Uruguay. I have also contributed to projects exploring the narrative innovations and marketing strategies adopted by Brazilian telenovelas and reality TV in response to market transformations.

I am very curious about the multiple ways in which culture, media, and people are interconnected. This curiosity drives a key strength of my work: engaging directly with primary and secondary sources across different markets, cultures, and languages, as I speak fluent English, Portuguese, and Spanish, and advanced Norwegian and French. I also enjoy sharing my knowledge, whether through teaching bachelor’s and master’s students, presenting at conferences, or writing laid-back non-academic texts.

Feel free to contact me if you want to discuss ideas or simply chat about TV!

Experience

 
 
 
 
 

Research Fellow

Department of Media and Communication (IMK) at the University of Oslo

Jan 2022 – Present Oslo, Norway
Developed a project on the transnational expansion of contra-flows of television content, with particular emphasis on the rise of Turkish dramas across Western European markets. For this project, I conducted interviews with audiences and television industry leaders across multiple countries and languages, including fieldwork at MIPTV (Cannes) and a research stay at the University Carlos III of Madrid. Currently, as a research fellow, I teach both bachelor’s and master’s level courses and supervise master’s theses.
 
 
 
 
 

Research Assistant

Ibero-American Observatory of Television Fiction (OBITEL/UFPR)

Mar 2020 – Jun 2021 Curitiba, Brazil
Collaborated on a project exploring narrative innovations in Brazilian telenovelas within the context of the COVID‐19 pandemic, focusing on how production shifted to adapt to safety restrictions and reflect societal concerns. In this project, I contributed to the qualitative analysis of Amor de Mãe (A Mother’s Love).
 
 
 
 
 

Research Fellow

Department of Communication (Decom) at the Federal University of Paraná

Apr 2019 – Jun 2021 Curitiba, Brazil
Conducted a comparative audience study on the expansion of Turkish content across Brazil and Uruguay, combining quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews in both countries to map emerging audience patterns. During this time, I was a member of the Center for Studies in Serial Fiction (NEFICS), which developed research on gender, identity and representation in serial fiction. Additionally, I contributed to multiple administrative tasks, such as organizing workshops for prospective students, managing the ENPECOM conference logistics (registrations, payments, and certificates), and compiling data on faculty, students, and research projects for the 2021 annual report to the Brazilian government.
